PROJECTS

Understanding the Impact of Ideological Bias on NLP Tasks

This project investigates the impact of ideological bias on Natural Language Processing (NLP) tasks, particularly in political news. Using word embedding techniques and language models like Word2Vec, GLoVe, ELMo, and BERT, the project aims to understand how biased news stories affect NLP tasks, focusing on topic classification. Information is gathered from major media's official Twitter accounts across the political spectrum (CNN, Fox News, Reuters), and a topic classification experiment is designed. By training state-of-the-art text classification models on diverse datasets and evaluating accuracy, the research contributes to our understanding of ideological bias effects on NLP models.

In response to the burgeoning importance of data in business operations, this project focuses on the evaluation of web scraping tools for extracting valuable insights from diverse online sources. I played a pivotal role in systematically surveying and analyzing various web scraping tools, considering metrics such as speed, accuracy, ease of use, and cost. This project explores cross-platform user behavior on social media platforms like Twitter, Instagram, and Mastodon. Disinformation Detection: An Evolving Challenge in the Age of LLMs

This project addresses the growing threat of disinformation using generative Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT. It investigates the effectiveness of current detection techniques against LLM-generated misleading content, explores the potential of using LLMs as a defense mechanism, and proposes novel approaches to counter this evolving threat.
